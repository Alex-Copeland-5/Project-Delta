What is Bakkes Mod?

BakkesMod acts as a locally run mod for Rocket League that injects new functionality into the game client, allowing for features such as custom overlays, aim trainers, and live performance metrics. Since all data remains on the client side, these modifications never alter gameplay or affect online opponents. Despite its flexibility, the existing interface is cluttered and unintuitive, making simple visual changes, like editing car overlays unnecessarily complex.

What does Bakkes Mod look like?

The BakkesMod menu pops up as a small, dark overlay window with several tabs running across the top. What is wrong with Bakkes Mod?

The current BakkesMod interface really shows its age, and it doesn’t match the cleaner, more intuitive design people expect from modern apps today. A lot of important features are spread out across awkward tabs, so you end up digging around just to find basic settings. It works, but it definitely feels like something built years ago that never got updated to match how people actually use it now.

The biggest problem I’m focusing on is the Items tab, which is the feature most people use. This section lets you equip any Rocket League cosmetic locally. This is a big deal considering most cosmetics are luck-based or locked behind a paywall. But even though it’s great for designing and previewing car setups, the menu itself is messy and tough to navigate. Everything feels crammed together, and finding specific items takes way more effort than it should. When you’re trying to look through hundreds of decals, wheels, boosts, and goal explosions, the layout starts to feel overwhelming fast.

How it could be better?

The two main issues with BakkesMod are its cramped, hard-to-use buttons and its lack of visual feedback. The current interface uses very small, tightly packed buttons that make navigating the menu harder than it should be. It’s easy to misclick or accidentally select the wrong item because everything feels squeezed together. This kind of layout might have worked years ago, but with how many features BakkesMod now has, the menu simply hasn’t kept up. A cleaner UI with larger, more spaced out buttons, ideally starting with a simple home page that shows all the main sections. This would make the entire mod feel much easier and more natural to use.

The other major issue is the lack of visual previews in the Items tab. The built-in Rocket League garage already shows a preview when you hover over a decal or cosmetic, but it only works for items you personally own. BakkesMod gives access to every cosmetic in the game, but you can’t see what something looks like until you actually click on it. When you’re browsing through hundreds of decals, wheels, paints, and goal explosions, having to equip each item one by one becomes slow and frustrating. Adding hover-based previews would make the customization process faster, smoother, and much more user friendly.
